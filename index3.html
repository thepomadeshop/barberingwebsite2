Barbers must use several tools to give the perfect haircut. First, there's the clippers, which remove the hair. Barbers typically have several clippers depending on the haircut being performed. Some popular ones are the Andis Fade Masters for fades, the Oster Classic 76ers for caesars, and the Andis Envy for mohawks.
For shape-ups and beard trims, the Andis T-outliner is typically the go-to tool. Many clients also like the Wahl Shaver Shaper for a very close cut. To get that "pop" factor for the line, most barbers use a razor. The razor is generally used after the Andis T-outliner, although it may be used in place of the outliner if the client has very little hair or hair that is not coarse.
Shears are used to blend the hair (for straight hair) and to remove strays. There are many types of shears: thinning, texturizing, etc. Make sure you are using the right kind of shear for the haircut that you want to achieve.
